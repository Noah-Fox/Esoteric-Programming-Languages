A Most Familiar Sequence.

Romeo, a young man boldly in love with Juliet.
Juliet, a young woman disinterested in her relationship.
Ophelia, an occasional lover of Hamlet.
Hamlet, a prince with a dead father.
Macbeth, an extravagent scottish lord.

	Act I: Exchanges between lovers.

	Scene I: Romeo and Juliet.

[Enter Romeo and Juliet]

Juliet: You are as fine as a tree. Listen to your heart.

[Exeunt]

	Scene II: Hamlet and Ophelia.

[Enter Hamlet and Ophelia]

Ophelia: You are as bold as a horse.

[Exeunt]

	Scene III: Hamlet's soliliquy.

[Enter Macbeth and Hamlet]

Hamlet: You are a brave good fine charming bold hero.

[Exeunt]

	Act II: The action.

	Scene I: The lovers make an appearance.

[Enter Romeo and Juliet]

	Scene II: A scandalous affair.

[Exit Romeo]

[Enter Hamlet]

Hamlet: You are as lovely as Ophelia. Open your heart!

[Exit Hamlet]

[Enter Ophelia]

Ophelia: You are as bold as the sum of yourself and Hamlet.

Juliet: You are as lovely as Hamlet.

[Exit Juliet]

[Enter Hamlet]

Ophelia: You are as beautiful as Juliet.

[Exeunt]

	Scene III: A shocking revelation.

[Enter Macbeth and Romeo]

Romeo: Speak your mind!

Macbeth: you are as large as the difference between yourself and a hero.

[Exeunt]

	Scene IV: A confrontation.

[Enter Romeo and Juliet]

Romeo: Am I better than the sum of a hero and a coward?

Juliet: If so, let us proceed to scene II.

[Exeunt]
